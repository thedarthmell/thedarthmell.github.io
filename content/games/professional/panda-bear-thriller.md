**Status:** Unreleased (Cancelled due to Studio Closure)  
**Role:** Lead Game Designer (Managed 1 Junior Level Designer)  
**Platform:** PC / Console (Planned)

### The Vision & My Role
When I joined **Panda Bear Entertainment**, the team had a concept for a psychological thriller involving "two different worlds" but lacked a cohesive structure to tie gameplay and narrative together.

As the **Lead Game Designer**, I took ownership of the project's direction for two years. My first major decision was to redefine the protagonist and the nature of these worlds to support a **Stealth-focused** gameplay loop.

* **Protagonist:** I proposed a 16-year-old girl to justify the stealth mechanics naturally珦he isn't a soldier; she is vulnerable and must hide to survive.
* **The "Two Worlds" Concept:** Instead of a generic alternate dimension, I grounded the duality in **Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID)**. 
    1.  **The Real World:** Harsh, physical reality where stealth is key.
    2.  **The Mind's Eye:** A warped, surreal projection of her psyche where the player solves puzzles.

### The Design: Architecting the Core Loop
I designed a cyclical core loop that made moving between these realities meaningful, rather than just a visual gimmick.

> **The Loop:** The player encounters a blocker in the Real World $\rightarrow$ Dissociates into the Mind's Eye to solve a logic puzzle representing her trauma $\rightarrow$ The solution alters the Real World $\rightarrow$ Player progresses using stealth.

**Key Responsibilities:**
* **System Design:** Built the rules for transitioning between reality and the mind.
* **AI Design:** Designed enemy behaviors and patrol patterns suitable for a non-combatant protagonist.
* **Level Design:** Supervised level blocking and flow; mentored a Junior Level Designer throughout the production pipeline.
* **Narrative Design:** Wrote the environmental storytelling elements that reflected the protagonist's fractured mental state.

### Post-Mortem: Why It Was Shelved?
This project was a labor of love for two years. Unfortunately, towards the end of 2023, the severe economic instability in T僡kiye caused a drastic currency devaluation. 

The studio, being self-funded without external investors, could not sustain the rising operational costs. The decision was made to close the studio and dissolve the project. While it is heartbreaking that *[PROJE ADI]* never saw the light of day, I am incredibly proud of the complex systems we built and the team I had the privilege to lead.
